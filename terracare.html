TerraCare

Role:

UI/UX, Visual Designer

Timeline:

Timeline: 2 months (Aug - Sep 2020)

Tools:

Tools: Sketch, InVision, UsabilityHub, Slack

Overview

With so many different recycling rules depending on where one lives, it can get confusing sometimes to follow best practices for sustainable waste management. To address this problem, TerraCare was designed to take into consideration the user's current location to provide simple, accurate, and personalized information.

It features three key functionalities: an intuitive search engine for items, a locator feature to find recycling centers or eco-friendly stores nearby, and useful articles and blogs geared towards every user's unique sustainable waste management goals.

Context

TerraCare is a sustainable waste management web app that I developed, from conception to execution, for my UI Design certification. The goal of the project was to design responsive screens that would function well and look beautiful across all breakpoints and devices. It also needed to follow conventional web app structure and considerations.

Competitive Analysis

The first step in my design process was to research similar sustainability apps that were currently in the market. This will allow me to identify their strengths and opportunities for my own app. I did an analysis of two competitors: RecycleCoach and How2Recycle.

User Personas

After completing a competitive analysis, I recruited and interviewed 5 people from the Slack community who were interested in sustainable living in order to better understand their needs and goals. I then created user personas based on my user research that helped me paint a clear and realistic picture of users’ goals, needs, and behaviors.

Minimum Viable Product

Next, I gathered requirements to define what features I needed to design for my app. I developed key user stories (from my personas) and minimum viable products (MVPs). The MVPs would allow me to quickly test my initial ideas and designs on users, giving me valuable feedback early in the design process and to address any concerns right from the beginning.

User Flow Diagram

Crazy 8's Wireframe

Using my user personas, user flows, and MVPs as a guide, I sketched out wireframes using the Crazy 8's method. After multiple sessions, I picked the designs that I felt addressed the MVP goals the best, and converted them to prototypes in InVision.

Prototype

User Testing

Usability Testing

Using my prototypes, I recruited 5 participants from the Slack community to conduct remote moderated usability testing on my designs. Afterwards, I synthesized and ranked my observations into concrete issues using Jakob Nielsen’s error severity rating system. This helped me understand what the pain points were and propose new solutions for my designs and ideas for my next iteration based on the results.

A/B Testing

Using UsabilityHub, I also conducted A/B testing to determine which typography style was preferred by users. With an 81% overwhelming majority, users voted that they liked the design to have a san-serif font over serif font, as it was more readable and matched the tone of an eco-friendly app better.
